Privacy Policy

Last updated: 22 July 2026

VidyaPath ("we", "the platform") is a free learning website for students. This policy explains what information we collect, why, and what choices you have.

1. Information we collect

When you create an account we collect:

  • Your name and email address — to identify your account and save your progress.
  • Optional profile details you choose to enter: college, city and degree/level. You may leave these blank.
  • Your learning activity — which lessons you complete, exercise attempts, saved notes, quiz results and any answers you save from "Ask Vidya".

If you sign in with Google, Google shares your name, email address and profile picture with us. We do not receive your Google password.

2. How we use it

We use this information only to run the learning service: to sign you in, save and show your progress across devices, and — if your school uses class features — to let your teacher see the work you have completed. We do not sell your personal information, and we do not show advertising.

3. "Ask Vidya" and AI providers

When you ask a question in the "Ask Vidya" feature, the text of your question, the subject and the level are sent to a third-party AI provider (currently Groq and/or Google Gemini) to generate the explanation. Do not include personal or sensitive information in your questions. Answers are cached so the same question is faster next time.

4. Cookies

We use a single essential cookie to keep you signed in. It contains a secure session token, not your password. We do not use tracking or advertising cookies.

5. Where your data is stored

Account and progress data are stored in a PostgreSQL database hosted on Railway. Data may be processed on servers outside India. We take reasonable measures to protect it, including hashed passwords and encrypted connections.

6. Children and schools

VidyaPath is designed for students, including those under 18. If you are under 18, please use VidyaPath with the involvement of a parent, guardian or teacher. Where a school enrols students, the school is responsible for obtaining any consent required from parents or guardians before students create accounts.

7. Your choices and rights

You can ask us to show, correct or delete your personal data at any time. To delete your account and associated data, or for any privacy question, contact us at the address below and we will action it.

8. Data retention

We keep your account data while your account is active. If you ask us to delete it, we remove your personal data from our live database.

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy. The "last updated" date above shows the latest version.
